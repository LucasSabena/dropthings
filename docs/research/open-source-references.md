# Open-Source References

## Scroll Direction

### LinearMouse

- URL: https://github.com/linearmouse/linearmouse
- License: MIT.
- Why it matters: strong reference for per-device mouse and trackpad settings, scroll direction, pointer speed, and acceleration.
- Use: study architecture and API approach. Do not copy code without tracking license and attribution.

### Scroll Reverser

- URL: https://pilotmoon.com/scrollreverser/
- Why it matters: focused app for independent scroll settings across trackpad, mouse, and Magic Mouse.
- Use: study behavior and UX.

### UnnaturalScrollWheels

- URL: https://github.com/ther0n/UnnaturalScrollWheels
- Why it matters: small focused solution for physical wheel inversion without changing trackpad behavior.
- Use: study minimal event-tap approach.

## File Shelf

### DropPoint

- URL: https://github.com/GameGodS3/Droppoint
- License: GPL-3.0.
- Why it matters: open-source cross-platform drag-and-drop shelf.
- Use: study UX. Be careful with GPL code compatibility.

### Dropp

- URL: https://github.com/spacefarers/Dropp
- Why it matters: modern shelf/dropzone concept.
- Use: study feature model and cross-device thinking.

### ShakePin

- URL: https://github.com/foamify/shakepin
- License: MIT.
- Why it matters: open-source Dropover-like utility with drag/drop, pinning, compression, and quick archive actions.
- Use: study interaction model.

## Menu Bar Management

### Ice

- URL: https://github.com/jordanbaird/Ice
- Why it matters: modern open-source menu bar manager with hiding, showing, Ice Bar, ordering, and appearance customization.
- Use: primary reference for feasibility and permission model.

### Hidden Bar

- URL: https://github.com/dwarvesf/hidden
- License: MIT.
- Why it matters: simpler open-source menu bar hiding utility.
- Use: study minimal implementation and UX.
